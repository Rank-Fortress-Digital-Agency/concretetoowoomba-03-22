THE THERMAL MASS OF CONCRETE

Thermal mass is the ability of a material to store heat. The thermal mass of concrete is one reason why it’s so popular for building construction, especially in hot climates. Concrete has an excellent thermal mass, which means that it can absorb a lot of heat without changing temperature significantly. That makes it ideal for capturing and storing daytime sunlight during the summer months and releasing that stored energy at night when the ambient air temperature drops below freezing (32°F). In other words, concrete acts as a buffer against extreme swings in temperature by absorbing warmth during the day and giving off warmth at night.
